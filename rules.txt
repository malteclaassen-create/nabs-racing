======================================

TIERS

We run two tiers, Tier 1 and Tier 2, plus reserves. A reserve can stand in for a
team when one of the regulars cannot make a race.


DRIVER STANDINGS

Everyone (Tier 1, Tier 2 and reserves) is in one big table, sorted by total
points. The points come straight from where the driver actually finished.


TIER 1 CONSTRUCTORS

For each team you just add up the real race points of its two Tier 1 drivers. If
a reserve drives for a Tier 1 team that race, his points count for that team.
Nothing gets re-ordered here, it is the real points.


TIER 2 CONSTRUCTORS

This one works a bit differently. For each race we keep only the drivers whose
team is a Tier 2 team and drop everyone else. Tier 1 drivers, and reserves who
are not driving for a Tier 2 team, are taken out completely - they do not hold a
spot. Then we re-order just those Tier 2 cars by where they finished and give
out points by that new order. So the Tier 2 points follow the Tier 2 finishing
order, not the overall one. A reserve driving for a Tier 2 team scores for that
team.


DROP SCORES (BEST 9 OF 12)

At the end, the three worst rounds of each driver and each team are dropped and
do not count toward the total. In a 12 race season that means your best 9
results count. A race you did not score in counts as a zero and is dropped
first. Rounds that have not happened yet also count as zero, so nothing real
gets dropped until late in the season. On the round by round tables the dropped
rounds are shown crossed out.


TIME PENALTIES

A steward penalty is added as seconds onto the driver's total race time (for
example 5 or 10 seconds), not as a number of positions. After the penalty the
car drops behind everyone it now sits behind on time. A car that was a lap down
or that retired is not affected, because it is not on the same time anyway. The
penalty gets entered in the admin and the order and the points update by
themselves.
