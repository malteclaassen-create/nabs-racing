NABS RACING LEAGUE - FEATURES
=============================

A rundown of what the site does. Everything on the public side comes out of a
database, nothing is typed in by hand on the pages, so when a race gets imported
every table updates on its own.


THE PUBLIC PAGES

Home
  The first thing you see. It shows the last race result (the podium and which
  track it was), who is leading the championship, who won the last race, and
  when the next race is with a live countdown. Under that you get the top of the
  driver table, both constructor tables (Tier 1 and Tier 2), and the points
  progression graphs.

Drivers
  The full driver standings, everyone sorted by total points. There is a filter
  to switch between all drivers, only Tier 1, only Tier 2 or only reserves, and
  a tick box to hide people who have not scored yet. Click a driver to open his
  profile.

Driver profile
  A stats page for one driver - wins, podiums, best and average finish, poles,
  places gained, a season form chart, a head to head against another driver,
  and a race by race table.

Constructors
  The Tier 1 and Tier 2 tables, both with a round by round points breakdown. The
  rounds that get dropped (see the rules file) are shown crossed out. Each tier
  also has its points graph and the team line-ups.

Races
  The full calendar. Finished rounds can be clicked and show the complete result
  - finishing order, the grid spot and how many places each driver won or lost,
  the fastest lap, and for Tier 2 a separate column with the re-ranked Tier 2
  position and points. Special events (like the Le Mans night) have their own
  tab.

Live timing
  This connects straight to our Assetto Corsa server and shows live sectors,
  best and last lap, gaps, tyres, pit stops and who is out on track right now.
  If no session is running it just says it is waiting for the server.

Sign up
  This is our replacement for Apollo. People log in with their Discord account
  and set themselves to Accepted, Declined or Tentative for each upcoming race.
  Every answer also gets posted and updated automatically in the Discord
  channel. While logged in a driver can also pick his own country here, and that
  then shows as his flag everywhere on the site.

Other things
  - Dark mode and light mode (button in the top right).
  - It can hold more than one season. Right now it is Season 7, but old seasons
    stay readable through a season picker once there is more than one.


THE ADMIN AREA

This sits behind a PIN (top right, "Admin"). From there I can:
  - Import a race. Either upload the result JSON from Content Manager or pull it
    straight off the server. It matches the in-game names to our drivers by
    itself and I just confirm them.
  - Edit any race afterwards. There is a live preview that shows exactly how a
    change moves the standings before I save it. This is also where I add
    steward time penalties.
  - Add and edit drivers and teams, including team colours and uploading team
    logos.
  - Manage seasons.
  - Set the Discord webhook and create or announce upcoming races and events.
  - Change the admin PIN.


HOW IT IS BUILT (short version)

The front end is React (Vite and Tailwind). The back end is Node and Express
with a Prisma database. Right now it runs on SQLite, which needs no setup, and
it can move to PostgreSQL when we put it on a real server. The sign-up login
uses Discord, and there is a Discord webhook for posting the attendance. All the
points and standings are worked out on the server, so the numbers are always the
same no matter where you look.
